The human impact on wildlife

Htet Eaindra Khaing | 16.5.2022

We all value our wildlife, who doesn't? However, it is us 'who value our wildlife' that has been demolishing our valuables.

Research indicates that biodiversity is vital for the processes that support all life on Earth, including humans. Without a wide range of animals, plants and microorganisms, we cannot have the ecosystem that we rely on to provide us with the air we breathe and the food we eat. That is not all, we rely on our vast ecosystems to provide services, such as pollination, soil fertility and stability. Our ecosystems weakened by loss of biodiversity are less likely to deliver those services we expect.

It is us that desperately needs wildlife to survive, although unfortunately it is also humans that have been threatening wildlife at the same time. The number of species in the wilderness are declining and the rapid loss of species we are seeing is estimated to be between 1,000 and 10,000 times higher than the natural extinction rate. We will be looking at the three main key threats in today's article.

Key Threats

  • Over exploitation
    •
  • Urban Development
    •
  • Poaching and Wildlife trade
    •

Over Exploitation

Over exploitation is the excessive use of natural resources at rates greater than they are able to replenish themselves, in other words, using resources at a faster pace than we should be. For instance, the harvesting of animals for meat, products such as horns and medicines, logging, and fishing; and it is the largest threat to wildlife and biodiversity. The problem of over exploitation has been aggravated by the exponential increase in human population, nearly 72% of 6241 species are under pressure from over exploitation.

Urban Development

Urban expansion threatens wildlife and assists climate change, it presents extreme challenges in biodiversity and ecosystem services. Humanity will urbanize an area of more than 460, 000 additional square miles and it could destroy more than 70,000 square miles of natural habitat by 2030. Our rapid expansion and even faster use of resources has destroyed vital wildlife habitat. If our current development and population growth continue unchecked, we will be losing our wildlife forever.

Poaching and Wildlife Trade

Poaching and wildlife trade remains one of today's major threats to the survival of wildlife species, particularly with the demand of animal parts for decorative purposes and for use in making remedies. It removes millions of animals from the wild annually, driven by high profit margins and high prices paid for rare species.
